Privacy Policy

Morning Brief · Last updated July 18, 2026

Morning Brief is a stock-chart briefing app. This policy describes what data the app handles and where it lives. The short version: your data stays on your device. We run no servers, require no account, and collect nothing ourselves.

Data stored on your device

Everything you configure in the app — your chart line-up, chart settings, display preferences, and refresh schedule — is stored only on your device. Cached market data (price history for the tickers you follow) is also stored only on your device. None of it is transmitted to us, because we have no servers to send it to. Deleting the app, or using Account → Reset App Data, permanently erases all of it.

Market data requests

To draw charts, the app requests price history for the ticker symbols you follow from market-data providers (Twelve Data and Alpha Vantage). These requests include the ticker symbols and your device's IP address, as any internet request does. They include no identity, account, or usage information. The providers' own privacy policies govern their handling of requests: Twelve Data, Alpha Vantage.

Purchases (Morning Brief Pro)

Subscriptions are processed by Apple through your App Store account; we never see your payment details. The app uses the Superwall SDK to present the upgrade screen and manage subscription status. Superwall receives anonymized device and subscription-status information to do this and does not use it to track you across other companies' apps. See Superwall's privacy policy.

What we don't do

  • No accounts, sign-ins, or profiles
  • No analytics or advertising SDKs
  • No tracking across apps or websites
  • No selling or sharing of personal data — we hold none to sell

Not investment advice

Morning Brief displays market data for informational purposes only. Nothing in the app is investment advice or a recommendation to buy or sell any security.
